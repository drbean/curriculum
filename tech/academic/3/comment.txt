   [36]Wikipedia has an article on:
   [37]comment
   [38]Wikipedia

Etymology[[39]edit]

   From [40]Middle English [41]coment, from [42]Old French [43]coment
   (“commentary”), from [44]Late Latin [45]commentum, from [46]comminisci
   (“to invent”).

Pronunciation[[47]edit]

     * ([48]UK) [49]IPA^([50]key): /ˈkɒmɛnt/
     * ([51]US) [52]IPA^([53]key): /ˈkɑmɛnt/
     *

       Audio (US)

       ([54]file)

Noun[[55]edit]

   comment (plural [56]comments)
    1. A spoken [57]remark.

                I have no comment on that.

          + 1907, [58]Robert William Chambers, chapter [59]IX, in The
            Younger Set ([60]Project Gutenberg; EBook #14852), New York,
            N.Y.: [61]A. L. Burt Company, published 1 February 2005
            (Project Gutenberg version), [62]OCLC [63]4241346:

                      “A tight little craft,” was Austin’s invariable
                      comment on the matron; and she looked it, always
                      trim and trig and smooth of surface like a converted
                      yacht cleared for action.

    2. ([64]programming) A remark in [65]source code which does not affect
       the [66]behavior of the program.

Verb[[257]edit]

   comment (third-person singular simple present [258]comments, present
   participle [259]commenting, simple past and past participle
   [260]commented)
    1. ([261]transitive) To remark.
          + 1908, [262]W[illiam] B[lair] M[orton] Ferguson,
            [263]Zollenstein, New York, N.Y.: [264]D. Appleton & Company,
            [265]OCLC [266]29686887 , chapter IV:

                      “My Continental prominence is improving,” I
                      commented dryly. ¶ Von Lindowe cut at a furze bush
                      with his silver-mounted rattan. ¶ “Quite so,” he
                      said as dryly, his hand at his mustache. “I may say
                      if your intentions were known your life would not be
                      worth a curse.”

          + 1915, [267]Emerson Hough, [268]The Purchase Price,
            [269]chapterI:

                      "A fine man, that Dunwody, yonder," commented the
                      young captain, as they parted, and as he turned to
                      his prisoner. "We'll see him on in Washington some
                      day. He is strengthening his forces now against Mr.
                      Benton out there. […]."

          + 2003 July 5, [270]Pierre Salinger, ABC News, “Analysis: Top
            film choices”, in NPR_Saturday:

                      I think Mamet always comments that commerce really
                      comes down to just a confidence game

          + 2009 Winter, John M. Kang, “Manliness and the Constitution”,
            in Harvard Journal of Law & Public Policy, volume 32, number
            1, page 261:

                      As Cambridge historian Mervyn James commented,
                      "silly quarrels escalated into battles in the
                      streets."

    2. ([271]intransitive, with "on" or "about") To make remarks or notes.
    3. ([272]transitive, [273]obsolete) To comment or remark on.

                (Can we [274]find and add a quotation of Fuller to this
                entry?)

    4. ([275]transitive, software, of code) To insert comments into
       ([276]source code).

                I wish I'd commented this complicated algorithm back when
                I remembered how it worked.

    5. ([277]transitive, software, of code) To [278]comment out (code); to
       disable by converting into a comment.
