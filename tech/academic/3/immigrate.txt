English[[20]edit]

Etymology[[21]edit]

   From [22]Latin [23]immigratus, past participle of [24]immigro (“remove,
   move into”).

Verb[[25]edit]

   immigrate (third-person singular simple present [26]immigrates, present
   participle [27]immigrating, simple past and past participle
   [28]immigrated)
    1. ([29]intransitive) To move into a country from another one to stay
       permanently.

Antonyms[[30]edit]

     * [31]emigrate
